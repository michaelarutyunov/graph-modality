Structural problems in the ontology

1. The relation schema makes topology nearly deterministic given node types.
Each relation type has a fixed source/target type signature: SERVES is always Construct→Value, EXPRESSED_VIA always Stance→Construct, etc. So once you
know the node-type multiset, the possible edge structure is heavily constrained — the graph is roughly a tripartite star pattern around Constructs. This
is why "C:V ratio" keeps showing up as the only discriminative structural feature: it's close to the only structural degree of freedom the ontology
allows. The "topology carries signal" hypothesis can't be fairly tested with a schema where topology is mostly a function of type counts.

Fix: add relations that create genuine topological variance between same-type nodes:
- SERVES chains or SUBSUMES: Value → Value (value hierarchies — laddering theory explicitly posits these)
- IMPLIES / CO-OCCURS: Construct → Construct (directed, distinct from CONFLICTS_WITH) — Kelly's personal construct theory treats construct systems as
networks, not flat lists
- Allow MODULATED_BY on Stances, not just Constructs

2. The max-2 CSM cap caused the H4 ceiling — and it's an arbitrary measurement clamp.
You diagnosed the ceiling yourself in Phase 4. Capping a count variable at 2 destroys exactly the variance H4 needed. The cap was presumably there to
prevent over-extraction, but a cap is the bluntest tool for that.

Fix: remove the cap; instead require each CSM to cite grounding spans from two different human turns (recurrence as evidence of "stable tendency"). That
controls over-extraction through evidence quality rather than truncation, and restores count variance.

3. Bipolarity-by-fiat caused the H3 ceiling.
"Must have both poles; if only one is inferrable, set bipolarity_complete to false" invites the LLM to invent the opposite pole (LLMs are excellent at
generating antonyms), inflating completeness toward ceiling. The flag measures DeepSeek's antonym fluency, not respondent bipolarity.

Fix: require a separate grounding_span for each pole. Bipolarity_complete = both poles independently grounded. This makes H3 testable again.

4. No intensity/centrality attribute.
A construct mentioned once in passing and one the respondent returns to five times are identical in the graph. Salience is arguably the most cognitively
meaningful weight you could attach, and it's cheap: count of supporting turns or spans.

Fix: require grounding_spans as a list and derive a salience weight; or add an explicit salience: peripheral|recurring|central attribute. This gives
weighted-graph features downstream.

5. The ontology is AI-evaluation-specific, undermining the CDT generality claim.
"Construct: a bipolar cognitive dimension used to evaluate AI's role" hardcodes the interview topic into the entity definition. A "distinct modality for
consumer digital twins" claim needs an ontology defined over the person, not over this dataset's subject matter.

Fix: reword to topic-neutral ("…used to evaluate the domain under discussion") and pass the domain as a parameter. Costs nothing now, and is required for
any second-dataset replication.

Prompt-engineering gaps (smaller)

- No target counts or stopping criterion. Mean 14.9 nodes/graph is whatever DeepSeek's verbosity prior produced. Different transcripts lengths will yield
different graph sizes for extraction-budget reasons, not cognitive ones — and graph size correlates with cohort if transcript length does. Fix: either
give a guideline ("extract every construct that meets the grounding test; do not limit count") or explicitly instruct exhaustiveness, then check
node-count vs transcript-length correlation as a confound.
- "Use sparingly" (CONFLICTS_WITH) is unquantified — classic deferred decision. Different model versions will interpret "sparingly" differently. Fix:
replace with an evidence criterion: "only when the respondent explicitly expresses tension between the two constructs; quote the span on the edge."
- No grounding for edges. Nodes need spans; relations don't. SERVES edges (Construct→Value) are pure inference and are the backbone of the graph. Fix:
require a grounding_span or at least a one-line rationale per edge — this is where extractor priors leak in most.
- No negative/empty-graph guidance. What should the model do with a transcript that's all logistics and no evaluative content? Without an explicit "it is
acceptable to return few or zero nodes," LLMs hallucinate to fill quota.
- Valence taxonomy: "mixed" vs "ambivalent" is a distinction most annotators (human or LLM) can't apply reliably without definitions and one example each.
Either define operationally (mixed = different valence toward different aspects; ambivalent = simultaneous conflicting valence toward the same aspect) or
collapse them.

Priority order

If you only do three: (1) per-pole grounding spans, (2) multi-span salience, (3) Construct↔Construct and Value→Value relations. The first two repair
H3/H4; the third is the one that gives the topology hypothesis a real chance — right now the schema largely guarantees that graph structure reduces to
node-type counts, which is exactly what your stats-only-≈-GIN-only results suggest.
